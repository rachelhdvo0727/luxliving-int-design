Overview

The best garden furniture will transform your outdoor space into a stylish and Instagram-ready haven for spring and summer 2021. From garden table sets and sun loungers to garden sofas, hanging egg chairs, outdoor rugs and the best garden furniture to leave outside - here are team GLAMOUR's top picks.

From reduced social interaction to health anxiety and the stress of uncertainty, there are a tonne of reasons why managing our mental health has been a little trickier during the global pandemic. Luckily, the one thing that eases all of the above is SUN - and there's an abundance of that just on its way.

Lockdown restrictions have eased and we can now spend long days outside sipping boxed wine or flavoured gin with our mates - but chances are your back yard is looking a little neglected after a year spent in lockdown. The best way to spruce up your garden is, of course, with the best garden furniture. Contrary to popular belief, you don't need a huge outdoor space (or great weather) to reap the benefits of the great outdoors. Plus, if you're living in a rented property and don't have much freedom when it comes to mixing up your interiors, transforming your outdoor area is a great compromise.

According to John Lewis, sales of outdoor cushions are up 66% compared to this time last year, so rest assured everyone else is trying to revamp their garden, too. Don't get left behind.

What is the best garden furniture to buy?

This totally depends on what you're after. The best garden furniture for you will depend on how big your space is, what you want to use it for and how many people you might be entertaining. If you've got an empty corner and lots more space, you might consider a corner sofa like this lounge unit from Made.com. We've also found tonnes of stylish sun loungers and inflatable pools to bring poolside chic to your back yard, plus garden parasols to protect you from the blazing sun. If you've just got a balcony to play with, a set of two outdoor dining chairs might be enough.

If we're talking about *quality* of garden furniture, there are a few sites we'd recommend you make a beeline for. Made.com is great for stylish small garden furniture that'll last for years, while John Lewis has an extensive offering - particularly when it comes to larger garden table sets and corner sofas, While Anthropologie is an absolute treasure trove of photogenic AF side tables, picnic blankets and parasols.

What type of outdoor furniture is the most durable?

Wrought iron garden furniture is generally considered to be the most durable kind of garden furniture, and it's luckily readily available. That said, all of the garden furniture in this edit has been built to last, as long as you look after it properly. This John Lewis bistro table and chairs set, for example, can be folded away to save space but also boasts a four-layer electroplated coating, giving it a tough and scratch resistant all-weather finish suitable for outdoor use all year round.

Which rattan garden furniture is best?

Ah, this question *always* comes up. We've narrowed it down to two favourites. The Meera Garden Corner Set is ideal for larger spaces and will fit all your guests comfortably. If you're doing up a balcony, this Calamus Rattan Chair comes in at a close second. It's durable and hardwearing but also fits into that trending Rattan theme.

We also love Made.com for Rattan-style garden furniture. Their Swara garden sofa is *chefs kiss* while their brand new Lyra hanging garden chair is to die for. Imagine kicking back with a new book in that?! They've also released a set of two Lyra armchairs to match - which cost less than £200 for the pair. You know what to do.
